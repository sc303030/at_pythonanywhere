Sundae

[순대]
Pork intestines stuffed with glass noodles, vegetables, sweet rice, coagulated pig's blood (seonji) and steamed.
Variations include Ojingeosundae (stuffed squid) and chapssal sundae (sweet rice sundae). This Korean blood sausage also comes in regional variations such as Byeongcheon or Abai Blood Sausage.

Which Korean alcohol do you like the most?

[막걸리] Makgeolli

Traditional Korean alcoholic beverage made by steaming glutinous and non-glutinous rice, barley, and wheat, mixing them with malt and water, and leaving the mixture to undergo fermentation.

[소주] Soju

Comparable to vodka but less potent, soju is the most popular traditional Korean liquor among the general public. Soju was originally brewed from grains; today it is mass-produced mainly from sweet potatoes.

[청주] Cheongju

Cheongju is a traditional Korean alcoholic beverage made from grain. It is made by saccharifying and fermenting rice with yeast, which is amalgamated and propagated by mold and yeast. It is also called clear wine or jingju (澄酒).
