Cooking DoenjangJjigae

  • 된장찌개

Doenjang-seasoned stew made with anchovy broth, fish or clams, and summer squash.
The broth is thick and flavorful, and good for mixing with rice.

Jjigae (stew) is made with various ingredients, such as meat, fish, clams, bean curd, and vegetables.
It is thicker than soups (guk or tang) and is served boiling hot.

SERVES
serves 4
PREP TIME
15 minute
COOK TIME
15 minute
Ingredients
  • .4 cups of deep anchovy meat juice (paper cup)
  • .1/2 potatoes
  • .1/2 onions
  • .1/4 spring onion
  • .1 shiitake mushroom
  • .1 quarter of green onion
  • .1/2 moss of tofu
  • .1/2 moss [seasoning] Doenjang 2 tablespoons (70~80g)
  • .0.5 large cayenne pepper
Directions
  • 01.Combine the potato, onion, zucchini, chili pepper, garlic, and shrimp in a 1½-quart (6 cups) earthenware pot or other heavy pot.
  • 02.Wrap the dried anchovies in cheesecloth (or a *dashi bag*, a pouch for stock-making sold at a Korean grocery store), and put them into the pot with other ingredients Add water and cover.
  • 03.Cook over medium-high heat for 15 minutes until it starts boiling. If you use a stainless steel pot, it will take less than 15 minutes, about 7 to 8 minutes.
  • 04.Stir in the soybean paste, mixing well. Cover and cook for 20 minutes longer over medium heat.
  • 05.Add the tofu and cook for another 3 minutes. Remove the anchovy pouch and discard.
  • 06.Sprinkle with the green onions and serve as a side dish to rice. Serve it directly from the pot, or transfer to a serving bowl. Everybody can eat together out of the pot, or portions can be ladled out in individual bowls for each person.
