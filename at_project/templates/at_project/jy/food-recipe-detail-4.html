Cooking DwaejiGogi KimchiJjigae

  • By Gildong Hong

Over-fermented kimchi boiled with pork, bean curd, and green onion. Often considered Korean “soul food,” this spicy kimchi stew strikes a perfect balance with the greasy pork.


Jjigae (stew) is made with various ingredients, such as meat, fish, clams, bean curd, and vegetables. It is thicker than soups (guk or tang) and is served boiling hot.

SERVES
serves 4
PREP TIME
15 minute
COOK TIME
15 minute
Ingredients
  • .2 cups of water (250 ml)
  • .250g for pork stew (or pork neck)
  • .200g for sour kimchi
  • .5 tablespoons of kimchi soup
  • .1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • .1/2 onion
  • .2 green peppers
  • .a little green onion

  • [meat seasoning]
  • .red pepper paste
  • .mirim (a Cooking wine)
  • .1 tablespoon of chopped garlic
  • .1/2 tablespoon soup soy sauce
  • .1/2 tablespoon of salt
  • .pepper paste
Directions
  • 01.Place the kimchi and kimchi brine in a shallow pot. Add pork and onion
  • 02.Slice 2 green onions diagonally and add them to the pot.
  • 03.Add salt, sugar, hot pepper flakes, and hot pepper paste. Drizzle sesame oil over top and add the anchovy stock
  • 04.Cover and cook for 10 minutes over medium high heat.
  • 05.Open and mix in the seasonings with a spoon. Lay the tofu over top.
  • 06.Cover and cook another 10 to 15 minutes over medium heat.
  • 07.Chop 1 green onion and put it on the top of the stew. Remove from the heat and serve right away with rice.
